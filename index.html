How much time does it take to beat the Game?

The average time to complete Getting Over It is around 3 to 5 hours for most players. However, this can vary significantly based on individual skill levels and familiarity with similar games. Some players may take longer, especially if they encounter difficult sections or need to practice certain techniques. On the other hand, experienced players or those who quickly adapt to the game's mechanics may finish in less time. Overall, the game's challenging nature means that completion times can differ widely among players.

Getting Over It with Bennett Foddy was developed by Bennett Foddy, an Australian philosopher and game designer. Foddy is known for creating challenging and often frustrating games that test players' patience and perseverance. He has a background in philosophy, which influences his approach to game design, often incorporating themes of struggle, failure, and human experience into his games.
In addition to Getting Over It, Bennett Foddy has developed other notable games such as QWOP, another notoriously difficult game that requires players to control a runner's legs using the Q, W, O, and P keys. Foddy's games have gained a cult following for their unique mechanics and the emotional responses they evoke from players.

The Scratch version of Getting Over It was created by "griffpatch", a well-known Scratch developer famous for creating high-quality games and tutorials on the platform. His adaptation brings the challenging mechanics of the original game to the Scratch platform, demonstrating the possibilities of what can be achieved within Scratch. The game has gained significant popularity in the Scratch community, showcasing griffpatch's skill in recreating complex game mechanics using Scratch's block-based programming.
